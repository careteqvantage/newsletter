BCBSM Pauses Planned 50% Cut to Same-Day E/M Reimbursement for Michigan Physicians
Michigan Healthcare Landscape · Reimbursement & PaymentHigh Priority

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan announced on April 15, 2026 that it is pausing the implementation of a new policy that would have reduced reimbursement by 50% for E/M codes billed with modifier 25 alongside minor procedures carrying 0- or 10-day global surgical periods. The policy—first announced in BCBSM’s February 2026 The Record newsletter—was originally set to take effect May 1, 2026, across all product lines including commercial, BCN, and Medicare Advantage. The pause follows coordinated advocacy by the Michigan State Medical Society (MSMS) and the Michigan Osteopathic Association, who cited payment accuracy concerns, operational challenges, and potential broad harm to healthcare delivery. BCBSM has not announced a revised implementation date.

Why It Matters: Michigan MSK and orthopedic practices routinely bill E/M services alongside minor office-based procedures such as joint injections, aspirations, and trigger point injections—exactly the billing pattern this policy targeted with a 50% reimbursement reduction. The pause removes an immediate revenue threat but the policy has not been permanently withdrawn, requiring active monitoring for a revised start date and defensible same-day documentation protocols.
  • Audit the last 90 days of claims to identify E/M + modifier 25 encounters billed with 0/10-day global procedures and quantify the revenue exposure if the policy is eventually reinstated with any revised implementation date.
  • Subscribe to MSMS Provider Alerts and BCBSM’s The Record newsletter to receive immediate notice of any revised implementation date or modified policy terms before the next billing cycle is affected.
  • Convene a billing and compliance review to ensure documentation clearly establishes the medical necessity and distinctness of same-day E/M services per AMA guidelines for modifier 25, so practices are audit-ready if the policy does return.

CMS TEAM Model Is Live: 741 Hospitals Now in Mandatory Orthopedic Bundled Payment Program
Regulatory UpdatesHigh Priority

The CMS Transforming Episode Accountability Model (TEAM) launched January 1, 2026 as a mandatory five-year bundled payment program (through December 31, 2030) covering three core orthopedic service lines: lower extremity joint replacement, surgical hip and femur fracture treatment, and spinal fusion—alongside coronary artery bypass graft and major bowel procedures. All IPPS hospitals in the selected Core-Based Statistical Areas (CBSAs) published in the FY 2025 IPPS Final Rule are required to participate with no opt-out provision, marking the first mandatory orthopedic bundled payment in CMS history. Episode pricing covers all costs from surgery through 30 days post-discharge including skilled nursing, home health, physical therapy, and follow-up visits.

Why It Matters: Orthopedic groups and ASC partners affiliated with TEAM-participating hospitals in designated Michigan CBSAs are now direct financial stakeholders in episode cost management—every SNF day, complication, and 30-day readmission affects the hospital’s TEAM reconciliation. Practices that can document superior outcomes, reduce unnecessary post-acute utilization, and collect patient-reported outcomes have a material competitive and contractual advantage in working with TEAM hospitals.
  • Confirm whether any hospital partner or referral source in your market is in a designated TEAM CBSA by consulting the CMS FY 2025 IPPS Final Rule CBSA list, and initiate care coordination discussions with their TEAM program managers immediately.
  • Implement or accelerate Patient-Reported Outcome Measures (PROM) collection pre- and post-operatively for joint replacement and spinal fusion patients—mandatory for TEAM participants and a differentiating data asset for any affiliated surgeon group.
  • Develop bundled care pathways for total knee, total hip, and spinal fusion episodes including SNF and home health utilization protocols specifically designed to contain 30-day post-discharge costs and reduce readmission risk within the TEAM episode window.

The Osteoboost device—the first and only FDA-cleared, non-drug treatment for low bone density—is worn over the sacrum and delivers low-magnitude, precisely calibrated vibration therapy targeting the lumbar spine and hips in postmenopausal women with osteopenia (T-score -1.0 to -2.5).
