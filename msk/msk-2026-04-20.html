CMS WISeR Model Activates 72-Hour Prior Authorization Review

🔴 HIGH Regulatory Updates

CMS's Wasteful and Inappropriate Service Reduction (WISeR) Model uses AI and machine learning alongside human clinical review to vet select Original Medicare items and services, including knee arthroscopy for osteoarthritis, electrical nerve stimulator implants, and skin/tissue substitutes. The 72-hour prior authorization turnaround requirement for requests sent to WISeR participant electronic portals applies starting January 5, 2026 for dates of service on or after January 15, 2026. The model runs for six performance years through December 31, 2031 in New Jersey, Ohio, Oklahoma, Texas, Arizona, and Washington — with Innovaccer serving as the Ohio participant.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK

Michigan is not currently a WISeR state, but the Ohio deployment on Michigan's southern border creates a direct test case for MSK prior authorization policy that CMS is expected to expand nationally. Michigan orthopedic leaders should evaluate WISeR workflow requirements now, since similar knee arthroscopy and spine-adjacent scrutiny is likely to reach Michigan payers in the next authorization cycle.

  • Audit knee arthroscopy for osteoarthritis case volumes, documentation patterns, and medical necessity rationale to benchmark against the WISeR evidence criteria likely to drive future Michigan coverage decisions
  • Assign a revenue cycle lead to track WISeR denial trends and appeals data published by CMS to identify documentation gaps that would expose Michigan practices in a future expansion
  • Prepare a prior authorization readiness assessment covering 72-hour turnaround capability, electronic portal integration, and human clinical review workflows in anticipation of broader CMS rollout
Effective Date: January 15, 2026 (dates of service) ⚠ Operational Trigger: January 5, 2026 — 72-hour turnaround begins

Site-Neutral Payments Redraw Orthopedic Economics in 2026

🔴 HIGH Reimbursement & Payment

Becker's ASC reports that CMS's CY2026 OPPS rule moves Medicare further toward site-neutral payment to narrow the reimbursement gap between hospital outpatient departments, ASCs, and physician offices — where hospitals currently receive about 60% higher payments for comparable services. CMS estimates the policy will reduce OPPS spending by roughly $290 million in 2026, including $220 million in Medicare savings and $70 million in beneficiary coinsurance savings. Industry leaders expect hospital payments to fall rather than ASC rates to rise, accelerating case migration, physician model renegotiation, and consolidation across orthopedic markets.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK

Michigan orthopedic groups affiliated with hospital outpatient departments face direct revenue compression as the 60% hospital facility-fee premium erodes, while independent orthopedic ASCs gain structural leverage in commercial contracting. Synergy Health and other Michigan MSK organizations should re-price their HOPD vs. ASC case economics now and prepare for employment model renegotiations through 2026.

  • Model the financial impact of site-neutral reimbursement on your top ten orthopedic procedures using a 60% reduction to HOPD facility fees as the baseline stress scenario
  • Evaluate partnership or joint-venture opportunities with independent Michigan orthopedic ASCs while HOPD valuations are repriced downward under site-neutral pressure
  • Engage payer contracting to audit commercial rate schedules against evolving CMS site-neutral benchmarks and renegotiate any clauses tied to hospital-rate parity
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 (CY2026 OPPS rule)

Adding Tibial Tubercle Osteotomy Fails to Improve Graft Survivorship

🟡 MEDIUM Technology & Innovation

New data presented at the AAOS 2026 Annual Meeting in New Orleans and published April 17 by Healio found that adding a tibial tubercle osteotomy (TTO) to patellofemoral osteochondral allograft transplantation did not improve graft survivorship and was associated with more healing complications. Two-year Kaplan-Meier graft survivorship was 95% overall, but reoperation-free survivorship was 67% in the TTO group versus 82% without TTO — a gap driven largely by TTO-related hardware complications. A total graft diameter greater than 27.5 mm was independently predictive of graft failure.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK

Michigan cartilage restoration programs and sports medicine surgeons should carefully weigh the added morbidity of augmenting patellofemoral allograft with a TTO given the absence of a survivorship benefit. This evidence supports tighter case selection and informed-consent documentation, and may influence payer coverage and ASC appropriateness criteria for combined procedures.

  • Update patellofemoral allograft informed-consent and indication templates to reflect the 2-year reoperation gap and the 27.5 mm graft diameter threshold for predicted failure
  • Circulate the Healio summary and AAOS abstract to Michigan cartilage-restoration and sports medicine surgeons for inclusion in the next departmental journal club
  • Review commercial and MA payer medical policies for combined TTO + OCA procedures and prepare to defend or revise protocols against evolving appropriateness criteria

2026 Medicare Fee Schedule Hides a 2.5% Efficiency Cut for Orthopedics

🟡 MEDIUM Reimbursement & Payment

Becker's Spine Review reports that the 2026 Medicare Physician Fee Schedule delivers a headline one-time 2.5% conversion factor increase effective January 1, but applies a 2.5% efficiency adjustment to non–time-based procedures that disproportionately hits orthopedic surgery, imaging interpretation, outpatient interventions, and interventional pain. Orthopedic surgeon compensation had already fallen 3% year over year after a 2.83% conversion factor reduction, with leaders calling the cumulative cuts unsustainable against 82% growth in labor and supply cost per FTE in physician-owned multispecialty practices from 2013 to 2022. CMS is also advancing the mandatory Ambulatory Specialty Model for orthopedics in designated geographies, with two-sided risk of up to 9% in year one and 12% later.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK

The net effect of the 2026 MPFS for Michigan orthopedic practices is likely a flat-to-negative Medicare revenue profile despite the advertised increase. Synergy Health and Michigan MSK groups should build the 2.5% efficiency adjustment into their 2026 revenue forecast and begin scenario planning for the Ambulatory Specialty Model's two-sided risk exposure.

  • Rerun the 2026 Medicare revenue forecast with the 2.5% efficiency adjustment applied to non–time-based orthopedic CPT codes and identify procedures where net reimbursement now falls below variable cost
  • Convene a risk-readiness working group to evaluate downside exposure under the Ambulatory Specialty Model and identify quality and cost benchmarks that must improve before mandatory participation
  • Accelerate a shift of eligible cases to ASC sites of service where commercial and Medicare economics outperform in-office or HOPD interventions
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
