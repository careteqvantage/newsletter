BCBSM Pauses Planned 50% Cut to Same-Day E/M Reimbursement for Michigan Physicians

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan announced on April 15, 2026 that it is pausing the implementation of a new policy that would have reduced reimbursement by 50% for E/M codes billed with modifier 25 alongside minor procedures carrying 0- or 10-day global surgical periods. The policy—first announced in BCBSM’s February 2026 The Record newsletter—was originally set to take effect May 1, 2026, across all product lines including commercial, BCN, and Medicare Advantage. The pause follows coordinated advocacy by the Michigan State Medical Society (MSMS) and the Michigan Osteopathic Association, who cited payment accuracy concerns, operational challenges, and potential broad harm to healthcare delivery. BCBSM has not announced a revised implementation date.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK Michigan MSK and orthopedic practices routinely bill E/M services alongside minor office-based procedures such as joint injections, aspirations, and trigger point injections—exactly the billing pattern this policy targeted with a 50% reimbursement reduction. The pause removes an immediate revenue threat but the policy has not been permanently withdrawn, requiring active monitoring for a revised start date and defensible same-day documentation protocols.
  • Audit the last 90 days of claims to identify E/M + modifier 25 encounters billed with 0/10-day global procedures and quantify the revenue exposure if the policy is eventually reinstated with any revised implementation date.
  • Subscribe to MSMS Provider Alerts and BCBSM’s The Record newsletter to receive immediate notice of any revised implementation date or modified policy terms before the next billing cycle is affected.
  • Convene a billing and compliance review to ensure documentation clearly establishes the medical necessity and distinctness of same-day E/M services per AMA guidelines for modifier 25, so practices are audit-ready if the policy does return.
Announced: April 15, 2026 Original Effective Date: May 1, 2026 (now paused indefinitely)

BCBSM–Michigan Medicine Standoff Risks Out-of-Network Break Affecting 300K Patients by July 1

Contract negotiations between Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan and University of Michigan Health (Michigan Medicine) have reached a critical juncture, with Michigan Medicine warning it will drop in-network status for BCBSM commercial plans on July 1, 2026 if a new agreement is not reached by June 30. The parties remain far apart: Michigan Medicine states BCBSM proposed a 30% reimbursement reduction, while BCBSM contends Michigan Medicine demanded a 44% increase over five years. More than 300,000 Michigan Medicine patients insured through BCBSM commercial plans—those not affiliated with the University of Michigan as employees or students—could be forced to seek care elsewhere. Medicare Advantage and Medicaid enrollees are excluded from the dispute. Both parties have stated commitment to resolution but no agreement has been announced as of April 2026.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK A July 1 BCBSM–Michigan Medicine out-of-network event would trigger significant referral pattern disruption across southeastern Michigan, particularly for complex joint reconstruction, spine care, and second-opinion pathways currently routed through UM Health. Independent MSK groups and orthopedic ASCs could see referral volume increases and payor mix shifts, while practices with their own BCBSM commercial contracts should understand their rate position as payer-provider tensions intensify statewide.
  • Map current referral relationships with Michigan Medicine across orthopedic, spine, and PM&R departments and model how a July 1 out-of-network event would redirect complex case volume to your practice or ASC.
  • Proactively communicate to BCBSM commercial patients that your practice remains in-network regardless of the Michigan Medicine dispute, reinforcing continuity of care messaging before any July transition occurs.
  • Review your own BCBSM commercial and Medicare Advantage contract terms and reimbursement rates to assess leverage and prepare for potential parallel reimbursement negotiations as payer-provider tensions intensify across Michigan.

CMS TEAM Model Is Live: 741 Hospitals Now in Mandatory Orthopedic Bundled Payment Program

The CMS Transforming Episode Accountability Model (TEAM) launched January 1, 2026 as a mandatory five-year bundled payment program (through December 31, 2030) covering three core orthopedic service lines: lower extremity joint replacement, surgical hip and femur fracture treatment, and spinal fusion—alongside coronary artery bypass graft and major bowel procedures. All IPPS hospitals in the selected Core-Based Statistical Areas (CBSAs) published in the FY 2025 IPPS Final Rule are required to participate with no opt-out provision, marking the first mandatory orthopedic bundled payment in CMS history. Episode pricing covers all costs from surgery through 30 days post-discharge including skilled nursing, home health, physical therapy, and follow-up visits. Three risk tracks apply: Track 1 (no downside risk for Year 1), Track 2 (lower risk/reward for safety net and rural hospitals), and Track 3 (full upside/downside risk for years 1–5).

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK Orthopedic groups and ASC partners affiliated with TEAM-participating hospitals in designated Michigan CBSAs are now direct financial stakeholders in episode cost management—every SNF day, complication, and 30-day readmission affects the hospital’s TEAM reconciliation. Practices that can document superior outcomes, reduce unnecessary post-acute utilization, and collect patient-reported outcomes have a material competitive and contractual advantage in working with TEAM hospitals.
  • Confirm whether any hospital partner or referral source in your market is in a designated TEAM CBSA by consulting the CMS FY 2025 IPPS Final Rule CBSA list, and initiate care coordination discussions with their TEAM program managers immediately.
  • Implement or accelerate Patient-Reported Outcome Measures (PROM) collection pre- and post-operatively for joint replacement and spinal fusion patients—mandatory for TEAM participants and a differentiating data asset for any affiliated surgeon group.
  • Develop bundled care pathways for total knee, total hip, and spinal fusion episodes including SNF and home health utilization protocols specifically designed to contain 30-day post-discharge costs and reduce readmission risk within the TEAM episode window.
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Program End: December 31, 2030

Intellijoint and Insurgical Partner to Bring Affordable Navigation Plus Single-Use Power Tools to ASC Joint Replacement

Intellijoint Surgical and Insurgical announced a strategic partnership on April 13, 2026, combining Intellijoint’s open-platform, surgeon-controlled navigation system for total hip and knee arthroplasty with Insurgical’s single-use, factory-tested, terminally sterilized powered instruments. Under the commercial agreement, Intellijoint’s U.S. sales team will jointly offer both product lines, addressing two core ASC adoption barriers simultaneously: the capital cost of robotic navigation and the logistical burden of powered instrument reprocessing. A co-development initiative will also explore integration of Intellijoint’s tracking technology with Insurgical’s next-generation power tool platform. Both companies designed this combination explicitly for the accelerating migration of joint replacement to ambulatory and cost-sensitive settings.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK Michigan orthopedic ASCs navigating the post-CMS ASC expansion into complex joint replacement face dual pressures: the expectation of navigation-quality outcomes and strict per-case cost containment. The Intellijoint-Insurgical combination removes the two most common capital and operational barriers to ASC navigation adoption without implant-system restrictions—particularly valuable for independent orthopedic ASCs not anchored to a major robotic platform vendor.
  • Request a product demo and cost comparison from Intellijoint’s U.S. sales team, benchmarking the bundled navigation plus single-use power tool pricing against your current instrumentation costs and sterilization overhead per case.
  • Confirm implant system compatibility before any trial commitment—Intellijoint operates on an open platform, but verify your specific hip and knee implant systems are supported by the navigation software prior to committing.
  • Identify 3–5 surgeons in your group performing total hip or knee cases in your ASC to pilot the combined platform over 90 days, capturing navigation accuracy data, turnover time, and total per-case cost for ROI analysis.

Osteoboost Raises $8M to Scale First FDA-Cleared Wearable Shown to Slow Bone Loss 85% in Spine

Osteoboost Health closed an $8 million financing round in April 2026, led by Ambit Health Ventures with participation from Emmeline Ventures, Disrupt Health Impact Fund, Esplanade Ventures, and Portfolia, to scale manufacturing and expand clinical research for its prescription wearable device. The Osteoboost device—the first and only FDA-cleared, non-drug treatment for low bone density—is worn over the sacrum and delivers low-magnitude, precisely calibrated vibration therapy targeting the lumbar spine and hips in postmenopausal women with osteopenia (T-score -1.0 to -2.5). A double-blinded randomized clinical trial demonstrated 85% slowing of spinal bone density loss and 55% reduction in hip bone density loss over 12 months of thrice-weekly 30-minute sessions. More than 2,500 physicians have prescribed the device since commercial launch, including providers at over 30 leading academic medical centers.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK Osteopenia and osteoporosis are direct upstream drivers of fragility fracture volume—a core surgical revenue stream for Michigan MSK practices. An FDA-cleared, non-pharmacologic device with validated bone-loss reduction data creates an opportunity to expand into bone health management as a preventive care line, positioning orthopedic surgeons as the prescribing specialist before fractures occur and establishing patient relationships earlier in the disease pathway.
  • Review your current fracture liaison service or osteoporosis care pathway and evaluate whether Osteoboost can be integrated as a non-pharmacologic adjunct for patients with osteopenia (T-score -1.0 to -2.5) prior to or following orthopedic procedures.
  • Contact Osteoboost Health to inquire about physician prescribing enrollment, institutional group prescribing accounts, and current payer coverage decisions for the device before recommending it to patients.
  • Identify your existing patient cohort with DXA-confirmed osteopenia who have not progressed to pharmacologic therapy—this is the primary prescribing target population and represents a new touchpoint for long-term orthopedic patient retention.

Five-Year Data Supports Implantable Shock Absorber as Durable Joint-Preserving Option for Medial Knee OA

A study reported April 21, 2026 by Healio Orthopedics presents five-year follow-up data showing that an implantable shock absorber system for medial knee osteoarthritis demonstrated sustained pain relief and functional improvement. Expert commentary characterized the device as “likely here to stay” and noted it “can be quite impactful for our patients going forward.” The findings were presented at the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons 2026 Annual Meeting in New Orleans. The device is designed as a joint-preserving surgical intervention positioned between conservative management and total knee arthroplasty for patients with unicompartmental medial knee OA who are not yet TKA candidates.

Why It Matters for Michigan MSK For Michigan MSK practices managing the growing cohort of medial knee OA patients who are too young, too active, or insufficiently symptomatic for TKA, durable five-year outcomes data for a joint-preserving implant expands the surgical menu and may support earlier surgical referral from non-operative MSK and PM&R specialists—creating new intermediate-level case volume while preserving the future TKA relationship.
  • Review the full Healio article and the AAOS 2026 Annual Meeting presentation for device-specific efficacy, safety, revision rates, and patient selection criteria before evaluating adoption readiness at your practice.
  • Identify current patients with isolated medial knee OA and activity profiles, age, or BMI parameters that make them poor TKA candidates—this is the primary clinical target cohort for initial adoption consideration.
  • Monitor CMS and BCBSM commercial coverage policies for implantable knee shock absorbers; five-year outcomes data typically triggers payer coverage reconsideration and should be tracked ahead of any patient counseling on this option.
